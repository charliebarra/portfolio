Story • 5 minute read

Curious about systems. Passionate about building better ones.

Looking back, I can see that each experience changed the questions I was asking. I stopped focusing only on how to play games and became more interested in how they were built.

Prologue • Ages 3–8

Where it started.

Some of my earliest memories are tied to games: Super Mario Bros., Disney Infinity, Mario Kart, Animal Crossing, and Exploding Kittens. At that age, I was not analyzing mechanics. I just knew I liked discovering what happened next, figuring out the rules, and trying different ways to play.

Before I wanted to design games, I wanted to understand everything they let me do.

Chapter One

Age 8 • 2018

Discovering Strategy

At eight, I was not thinking about “systems design.” I just knew I liked games that gave me a reason to try again. Pokémon made me pay attention to planning and prediction, and I started noticing that different players could use completely different strategies.

Games became more interesting when I started asking why one choice worked better than another.

Chapter Two

Age 9 • 2019

Discovering Competition

Traveling to major tournaments showed me that games create communities as much as competition. I met players who approached the same cards and matchups in completely different ways, and I learned that preparation only works if you can also adapt.

There is never just one way to solve a problem.

Chapter Three

Age 10 • 2020

Learning from Setbacks

I qualified for the Pokémon World Championships in London, but the event was cancelled during the COVID-19 pandemic. It was disappointing, but it taught me that one cancelled event did not erase the work it took to qualify.

Keep building, even when the path changes.

Chapter Four

Ages 11–12 • 2021–2022

Becoming Curious

When competitive play returned, I noticed I was asking different questions. I still wanted to win, but I also wanted to understand why certain strategies became popular, how counters developed, and how small rule changes affected the whole game.

I became as interested in understanding the game as I was in winning it.

Chapter Five

Ages 13–14 • 2023–2024

From Player to Builder

Roblox and Minecraft gave me places to build, experiment, and make my own versions of the experiences I liked. I was actively creating in both, and I began paying attention to world layout, atmosphere, interaction, and what made players want to keep exploring.

The best way to understand a system is to try building one.

Chapter Six

Age 15 • 2025

Designing My Own Game

Meteor Mayhem was my first attempt to design a full strategy game from the ground up. I created the rules, cards, resources, upgrades, and hazards, then tested the game with friends, classmates, family, and members of the local gaming community.

Iteration is where ideas become experiences.

Chapter Seven

Senior Year • Today

Just Getting Started

In August 2025, I began the Cybersecurity & Information Protection share-time program during my junior year. Since then, I have been learning programming, cybersecurity, and technical problem-solving while continuing to explore game design and interactive systems.

Curious about systems. Passionate about building better ones.

Continue exploring.

Every project in this portfolio represents another step in that journey. Some began as questions. Others began as experiments. All of them helped me understand how thoughtful design shapes the way people think, learn, and play.
