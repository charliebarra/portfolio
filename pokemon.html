Competitive Play

Pokémon started as a game I loved playing with my dad. Over time, it became the first place I learned how probability, preparation, adaptation, and decision-making work inside a competitive system.

Timeline

Six years of competition, travel, losses, testing, and improvement.

What it taught me

Competitive play became my first design classroom.

Probability

Every match involves risk, odds, draw probabilities, and decisions made with incomplete information.

Adaptation

As players solve one strategy, new counters emerge. The game keeps changing because the community keeps changing.

Preparation

Testing, matchup study, repetition, and learning from losses matter more than simply knowing the rules.

Resilience

Competitive games teach you to lose, adjust, and try again without pretending every match went perfectly.

Systems Thinking

Small card changes can reshape an entire metagame, which made me more interested in how design choices affect player behavior.

Community

Traveling to events helped me see games as communities, not just products. Strategy develops through conversation, testing, and shared experience.
