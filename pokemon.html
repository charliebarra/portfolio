Competitive Play

What Pokémon taught me about patterns, preparation, and being wrong.

Pokémon started as a game I loved playing with my dad. Competitive play made me pay attention to probability, preparation, and patterns. It also taught me that a plan can be very good and still be wrong by the second turn.

Competitive Systems

Preparation matters. Then the match actually starts.

Probability

Every decision involves information I have, information I do not have, and several things that could happen next.

Adaptation

A strategy can be great one week and much worse the next because everyone starts preparing for it.

Resilience

A loss is useful when I can figure out what I misunderstood, missed, or should prepare for next time.

Community

Traveling and competing introduced me to people who looked at the same cards and found completely different answers.

Connection to Design

Players change a game by figuring it out.

Competitive Pokémon taught me that balance is not only math. Players share ideas, find counters, and change what everyone else has to prepare for. I am still curious how designers balance a game when thousands of players are trying to solve it at the same time.
