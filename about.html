About

Curious about systems.

I’m Charlie. I like game design, programming, cybersecurity, and interactive systems — especially projects where small design choices change how people think, play, and solve problems.

From player to designer

I started by playing games. Then I started asking why they worked.

Competitive Pokémon taught me about probability, preparation, matchups, risk, and adaptation. Roblox showed me that players could also become creators. Cybersecurity made me think about how systems break and how people fix them. Meteor Mayhem helped me put those ideas into a game of my own.

How I work

Build, test, learn, revise.

Start with curiosity

I usually begin by asking what makes a mechanic, system, or interaction interesting.

Make something playable

I learn more from a rough prototype than from keeping an idea perfect in my head.

Watch what breaks

Testing shows where the system is confusing, too random, too powerful, or just not fun.

Use feedback

Other players notice things I miss. Their choices help me understand the system better.

Think about balance

I like the challenge of making games feel strategic without making them predictable.

Keep improving

The best version of a project usually comes after several versions that did not work.
