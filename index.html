My Story

I didn’t set out to become a game designer.

I started as a competitive player, became fascinated by why games work, and eventually began building systems of my own.

Age 3–8

Games like Super Mario Bros. first made me curious about challenge, reward, and why some games keep pulling players back.

Age 7–10

Roblox and Minecraft helped me see games as places where players could also become creators.

Age 9–10

Competitive Pokémon taught me that preparation, probability, and adapting to opponents matter.

Age 15

I designed Meteor Mayhem, a strategy card game, from rough ideas to a playable prototype.

Age 15–17

Cybersecurity and programming helped me understand how complex systems work, fail, and get fixed.

Next

I’m building Python games, simulation tools, and interactive learning systems.
